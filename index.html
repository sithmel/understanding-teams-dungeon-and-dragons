D&D classes and team roles

In D&D every player has a specific class: warriors, wizard, paladin, thief etc. Adventures are often designed in such a way to make teamwork and collaboration across different characters and classes necessary to be successful.

Roles cover these areas:

  • Result
  • Methodology
  • Innovation
  • Communication
In any type of teams there are implicit or explicit "roles" covering specific areas: In a team a person discover their real value, through the difference with the other members.
In an healthy team everyone has one or more roles

Team characteristics

In D&D each player has a sheet with their character's stats: like intelligence, strength, dexterity, wisdom etc. Team characteristics are like that, but measured on a team. This is another useful model that can be used to identify team issues.

Rate your team from 1 to 10!

Institutional leadership

vs

Functional leadership

Institutional leader

The institutional leader behaves more as a dungeon master. They describes "reality" to their team "translate" the confusing messages that come from the organisation in a way that is coherent and unambiguous. The leader bring order to chaos, and take their team together in a journey to achieve the team goals. Said in another way: the leader takes care of setting the context in which the team is working. They set expectations for the team members and the team members ask their support to meet the expectations. A dungeon master takes care that the game is enjoyable by their players!

Functional leader

A D&D party can function at its best not just through individual initiative, but also through coordination of their actions. In a group of people this is facilitated by a leader. This can emerge naturally or instituted by the organisation.
Functional leadership
One or more leaders are chosen by the team based on their competence. They can change over time, as they are the result of a negotiation. They are chosen when the members feel the necessity to be led. The istitutional leader is not enough for covering all team needs.
